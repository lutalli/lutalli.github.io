> {{ "DEF-RH:Ring Homomorphism" | codexify }}
>
> Let $R$ and $S$ be two [rings](/codex/RING). A **ring [homomorphism](/codex/MOR)** between $R$ and $S$ is a [function](/codex/F) $f:R\to S$ such that:
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. For all $a$, $b\in R$,
>
>    $$ f(a+b) = f(a)+f(b). $$
>
> 2. For all $a$, $b\in R$,
>
>    $$ f(a\cdot b) = f(a)\cdot f(b). $$
>
> 3. $$ f(1) = 1. $$
>
> Namely, $f$ is both a [group homomorphism](/codex/GH) from $(R,+)$ to $(S,+)$ and a [monoid homomorphism](/codex/MOH) from $(R,\cdot)$ to $(S,\cdot)$.

> {{ "DEF-RH-TRI:Trivial Ring Homomorphism" | codexify }}
>
> The **trivial / zero ring homomorphism** between two rings $R$ and $S$ is the function
>
> $$ f : R\to S, \, a\mapsto 0. $$

> {{ "DEF-RH-KER:Kernel" | codexify }}
>
> The **kernel** of a ring homomorphism $f:R\to S$ is
>
> $$ \ker f = f^{-1}\big[\{0\}\big]. $$

---

> {{ "PROP-RH-A" | codexify }}
>
> Let $f:R\to S$ is a ring homomorphism.
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. $f(0)=0$.
> 2. $f(-a)=-f(a)$ for every $a\in R$.

**Proof.**{:.run-in}By [PROP-GH-A](/codex/GH#PROP-GH-A).<qed />

> {{ "PROP-RH-MON" | codexify }}
>
> Let $f:R\to S$ be a ring homomorphism. $f$ is a [monomorphism](/codex/MOR) if and only if
>
> $$ \ker f = \{0\}. $$

**Proof.**{:.run-in}By [PROP-GH-MON](/codex/GH#PROP-GH-MON).<qed />
