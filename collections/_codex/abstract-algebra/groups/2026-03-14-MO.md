> {{ "DEF-MO:Monoid" | codexify }}
>
> A **monoid** is a [unital](/codex/MAG#DEF-MAG-NEU) [semigroup](/codex/SEM).
>
> Namely, a monoid is a [magma](/codex/MAG) $(M,\*)$ that satisfies the following properties:
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. *Associativity.* For all $a$, $b$, $c\in M$,
>
>    $$ a(bc) = (ab)c. $$
>
> 2. *Neutral Element.* There exists $e\in M$ such that for all $x\in M$,
>
>    $$ ex = xe = x. $$

---

> {{ "PROP-MO-P" | codexify }}
>
> A monoid is [uniquely unital](/codex/MAG#DEF-MAG-NEU).

**Proof.**{:.run-in}Let $(M,\*)$ be a monoid. If both $e$ and $e'$ are neutral elements of $M$,

$$ e = ee' = e'. $$

&#8203;<qed />

> {{ "PROP-MO-LR" | codexify }}
>
> Let $(M,\*)$ be a monoid and $x\in M$. $x$ is left-invertible if and only if $x$ is right-invertible.

**Proof.**{:.run-in}Let $e$ be the neutral element and $x\in M$.

{:.no-indent}
- If $x$ is left-invertible, then there is $y\in M$ such that $xy=e$. We have

  $$ x(yx) = (xy)x = ex = x, $$

  which follows that $yx=e$.

- If $x$ is right-invertible, then there is $y\in M$ such that $yx=e$. We have

  $$ (xy)x = x(yx) = xe = x, $$

  which follows that $xy=e$.<qed />

> {{ "PROP-MO-SUB" | codexify }}
>
> Let $(M,\*)$ be a monoid. $N\subseteq M$ is a [submonoid](/codex/SUBS) as soon as
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. $\*$ is [closed](/codex/OCL) in $N$;
>
> 2. $N$ is [unital](/codex/MAG#DEF-MAG-NEU).

> {{ "REM-MO-SUB" | codexify }}
>
> If $N$ is a submonoid of $M$, $N$ does not have to share the same neutral element as $M$.

**Example.**{:.run-in}$(\N,\max)$ is a monoid with neutral element $0$ and $(\N^*,\max)$ is a submonoid with neutral element $1$.

> {{ "DEF-MO-HOM:Monoid Homomorphism" | codexify }}
>
> A **monoid [homomorphism](/codex/MOR)** between two monoids $(M,\*)$ and $(N,\cdot)$ is a [function](/codex/F) $f:M\to N$ such that for all $a$, $b\in M$,
>
> $$ f(a*b) = f(a)\cdot f(b). $$
