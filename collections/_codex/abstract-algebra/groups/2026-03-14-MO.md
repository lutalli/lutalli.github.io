> {{ "DEF-MO:Monoid" | codexify }}
>
> A **monoid** is a [unital](/codex/MAG#DEF-MAG-NEU) [semigroup](/codex/SEM).

---

> {{ "PROP-MO-P" | codexify }}
>
> A monoid is [uniquely unital](/codex/MAG#DEF-MAG-NEU).

**Proof.**{:.run-in}Let $M$ be a monoid. If both $e$ and $e'$ are neutral elements of $M$,

$$ e = ee' = e'. $$

&#8203;<qed />

> {{ "PROP-MO-LR" | codexify }}
>
> Let $M$ be a monoid and $a\in M$. $a$ is left-invertible if and only if $a$ is right-invertible.

**Proof.**{:.run-in}Let $e$ be the neutral element.

{:.no-indent}
- If $a$ is left-invertible, then there is $x\in M$ such that $xa=e$. We have

  $$ x(ax) = (xa)x = ex = x, $$

  which follows that $ax=e$.

- If $a$ is right-invertible, then there is $x\in M$ such that $ax=e$. We have

  $$ (xa)x = x(ax) = xe = x, $$

  which follows that $xa=e$.<qed />

> {{ "PROP-MO-SUB" | codexify }}
>
> Let $(M,\*)$ be a monoid. $N\subseteq M$ is a [submonoid](/codex/SUBS) as soon as
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. $N$ is [closed](/codex/OCL) under $\*$;
> 2. $N$ is [unital](/codex/MAG#DEF-MAG-NEU).

> {{ "REM-MO-SUB" | codexify }}
>
> A submonoid does not always inherit the neutral element.

**Example.**{:.run-in}$(\N,\max)$ is a monoid with neutral element $0$, but $(\N^*,\max)$ is a submonoid with neutral element $1$.

---

> {{ "DEF-MO-HOM:Monoid Homomorphism" | codexify }}
>
> A **monoid [homomorphism](/codex/MOR)** between two monoids $(M,\*)$ and $(N,\diamond)$ is a [function](/codex/F) $f:M\to N$ such that for all $a$, $b\in M$,
>
> $$ f(a*b) = f(a)\diamond f(b). $$
