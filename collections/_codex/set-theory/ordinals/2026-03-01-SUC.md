> {{ "DEF-SUC:Ordinal Successor" | codexify }}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - The **successor** of an [ordinal](/codex/ORD) $\alpha$ is
>
>   $$ \suc\alpha = \alpha\cup\{\alpha\}, $$
>
>   which is also an ordinal.
>
> - $\alpha$ is a **successor ordinal**, if $\alpha=\suc\beta$ for some ordinal $\beta$.

---

> {{ "PROP-SUC-INF" | codexify }}
>
> For any ordinal $\alpha$,
>
> $$ \suc\alpha = \inf\{\beta:\beta>\alpha\}. $$

> {{ "PROP-SUC-CARD" | codexify }}
>
> For any [infinite ordinal](/codex/N) $\alpha$,
>
> $$ \suc\alpha \approx \alpha. $$

**Proof.**{:.run-out}

{:.no-indent}
- Since $\alpha\subseteq\suc\alpha$, $\alpha\lesssim\suc\alpha$ by [PROP-EQU-SUB](/codex/EQU#PROP-EQU-SUB).

- Define

  $$ f : \suc\alpha\to\alpha,\enspace \xi\mapsto\begin{cases}
      0, & \text{if $\xi=\alpha$}; \\
      \suc\xi, & \text{if $\xi<\omega$}; \\
      \xi, & \text{otherwise}.
  \end{cases} $$

  Then $f$ is injective, so $\suc\alpha\lesssim\alpha$.

By [SchrĂ¶der-Bernstein Theorem](/codex/EQU#PROP-EQU-SB), $\suc\alpha\approx\alpha$.<qed />
