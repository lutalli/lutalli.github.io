> {{ "DEF-SUC:Ordinal Successor" | codexify }}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - The **successor** of an [ordinal](/codex/ORD) $\alpha$ is the ordinal
>
>   $$ \alpha+1 = \alpha\cup\{\alpha\}. $$
>
> - $\alpha$ is a **successor ordinal**, if $\alpha=\beta+1$ for some ordinal $\beta$.

---

> {{ "PROP-SUC-INF" | codexify }}
>
> For any ordinal $\alpha$,
>
> $$ \alpha+1 = \inf\{\beta:\beta>\alpha\}. $$

> {{ "PROP-SUC-CARD" | codexify }}
>
> For any [infinite ordinal](/codex/N) $\alpha$,
>
> $$ \lvert\alpha+1\rvert = \lvert\alpha\rvert. $$

**Proof.**{:.run-out}

{:.no-indent}
- Since $\alpha\subseteq\alpha+1$, $\lvert\alpha\rvert\leq\lvert\alpha+1\rvert$ by [PROP-EQU-SUB](/codex/EQU#PROP-EQU-SUB).

- Define

  $$ f : \alpha+1\to\alpha, \, \xi\mapsto\begin{cases}
      0, & \text{if $\xi=\alpha$}; \\
      \xi+1, & \text{if $\xi<\omega$}; \\
      \xi, & \text{otherwise}.
  \end{cases} $$

  Then $f$ is injective, so $\lvert\alpha+1\rvert\leq\lvert\alpha\rvert$.

By [Schröder-Bernstein Theorem](/codex/EQU#PROP-EQU-SB), $\lvert\alpha+1\rvert=\lvert\alpha\rvert$.<qed />
