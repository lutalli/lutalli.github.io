> {{ "DEF-F:Function" | codexify }}
>
> A **function** / **mapping** / **transformation** is a [binary relation](/codex/R) that is [functional](/codex/RCL), i.e. a binary relation $f$ such that for any $x$, $y$, $z\in\dom f$,
>
> $$ (z,x)\in f \,\land\, (z,y)\in f \enspace\rightarrow\enspace x=y. $$
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - For any $x\in\dom f$, the **value** of $f$ at $x$, written as $f(x)$, is the unique $y$ such that $(x,y)\in f$. Another notation for $f(x)=y$ is
>
>   $$ f : x\mapsto y. $$
>
> - $f$ is **onto** $Y$, if $\ran f=Y$.
>
> - $f$ is **from** $X$ **to** / **into** $Y$, written
>
>   $$ f : X\to Y, $$
>
>   if $\dom f=X$ and $\ran f\subseteq Y$. In this context, $Y$ is called the **codomain** or **set of destination** of $f$.
>
> - $f$ is **on** $X$, if $f:X\to X$.
>
> - An **$n$-ary operation** on $X$ is a function $\*:X^n\to X$.
>
>   If $\*$ is a binary operation, we write
>
>   $$ x*y \quad\text{for}\quad *(x,y). $$
>
> - The set of all functions from $X$ to $Y$ is denoted by $Y^X$. This is a set:
>
>   $$ Y^X \subseteq \powerset(X\times Y). $$
