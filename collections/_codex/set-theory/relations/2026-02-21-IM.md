> {{ "DEF-IM:Image" | codexify }}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - Let $R$ be a [binary relation](/codex/R). If $A\subseteq\dom R$, the **image** of $A$ under $R$ is
>
>   $$ R[A] = \{y:(\exists x\in A:x\,R\,y)\}. $$
>
>   This is a set:
>
>   $$ R[A] \subseteq \ran R. $$
>
> - The **image** of $R$ is just a synonym for its range:
>
>   $$ \im R = \ran R. $$
>
> - If $B\subseteq\ran R$, the **inverse image** / **preimage** of $B$ under $R$ is $R^{-1}[B]$, where $R^{-1}$ is the [converse](/codex/CV) of $R$.

---

> {{ "PROP-IM-EMP" | codexify }}
>
> For any binary relation $R$,
>
> $$ R[\varnothing] = \varnothing. $$
