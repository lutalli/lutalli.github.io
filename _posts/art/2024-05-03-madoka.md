[Madoka Runes](https://wiki.puella-magi.net/Witch_Runes) are the runes occured in the anime [Puella Magi Madoka Magica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puella_Magi_Madoka_Magica), originally created by [Gekidan Inu Curry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gekidan_Inu_Curry). They cover the [German alphabet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_alphabet), which includes A, B, ..., Z, Ä, Ö, Ü, ß and the numerals 0, 1, ... 9. Below is my own handwritten version.

## Remarks

{: .no-indent }
- Compared to the original design of the letter [Bb], the "+" inside the ring has been simplified to a dot.

- The lowercase forms of some letters are vertically extended, e.g. [Hh], [Oo], [Pp], [Ww]. This is comparable to Latin letters like Hh, Jj, Ll, Pp.

- In some cases the regular lowercase form is not vertically extended, but its italic variation is, e.g. [Gg], [Ii], [Ll], [Rr].

- The original design of [Ss] consists of three vertically aligned dots, which looks like a vertical ellipsis. Since dots and ellipsises appear quite often in mathematical writing, it has been slightly redesigned to avoid visual ambiguity.

- The stick-like letter [Vv] has its italic form flipped so that it shares the same slope as other italic letters.

- Two different designs are provided for the lowercase letter [x]. The second variant shall be used for an [x] positioned at the end of a word, e.g. *fox*, *matrix*. This is comparable to the Greek letter σ/ς. It is also recommended to use the second variant for the mathematical variable $x$.

- Compared to the original design of the numeral [0], the "×" inside the egg has been simplified to a dot.

- Other variants like bold and double-struck can be easily improvised, e.g. $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{N}$ in the examples above.
