> **DEF-BLD. Set-Builder.**{: .codex-h }
>
> {: .no-indent }
> - Let $X$ be a set and $\varphi(x,p)$ be a formula with free variables among $x$ and $p$. The notation
>
>   $$ \{x\in X : \varphi(x,p)\} $$
>
>   denotes the unique set $Y$ such that
>
>   $$ \forall x :\enspace x\in Y \enspace\Leftrightarrow\enspace x\in X\land\varphi(x,p). $$
>
>   The existence of $Y$ is justified by [Separation Schema](/codex/ZF#AX-ZF-SEP) and the uniqueness is justified by [Axiom of Extensionality](/codex/ZF#AX-ZF-EXT).
>
> - If for some set $X$
>
>   $$ \forall x :\enspace \varphi(x,p) \enspace\Rightarrow\enspace x\in X, $$
>
>   then
>
>   $$ \forall x :\enspace x\in X\land\varphi(x,p) \enspace\Leftrightarrow\enspace \varphi(x,p). $$
>
>   Therefore, by Separation Schema and Axiom of Extensionality, there is a unique set $Y$ such that
>
>   $$ \forall x :\enspace x\in Y \enspace\Leftrightarrow\enspace \varphi(x,p). $$
>
>   In this case, we can simply write
>
>   $$ Y = \{x:\varphi(x,p)\}. $$
