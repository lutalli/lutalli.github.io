> <span class="math-h">DEF-SUB. Subset.</span>
>
> $X$ is a **subset** of $Y$, written $X\subseteq Y$, if
>
> $$ \forall x :\enspace x\in X \enspace\Rightarrow\enspace x\in Y. $$
> 
> $X$ is a **proper subset** of $Y$, written $X\subset Y$, if $X\subseteq Y$ and $X\neq Y$.

> <span class="math-h">PROP-SUB-EMP.</span>
>
> $\varnothing$ is the only set that is a subset of any set. That is, for any $X$,
>
> $$ X=\varnothing \enspace\Leftrightarrow\enspace \forall Y:X\subseteq Y. $$

> <span class="math-h">PROP-SUB-REF. Reflexivity of $\subseteq$.</span>
>
> For any $X$,
>
> $$ X\subseteq X. $$

> <span class="math-h">PROP-SUB-TR. Transitivity of $\subseteq$.</span>
>
> For any $X$, $Y$ and $Z$,
>
> $$ X\subseteq Y\land Y\subseteq Z \enspace\Rightarrow\enspace X\subseteq Z. $$

> <span class="math-h">PROP-SUB-ATSYM. Antisymmetry of $\subseteq$.</span>
>
> For any $X$ and $Y$,
>
> $$ X\subseteq Y\land Y\subseteq X \enspace\Rightarrow\enspace X=Y. $$

> <span class="math-h">PROP-SUB-PO.</span>
>
> For any $X$, if we let
>
> $$ {\subseteq_X} = \{(x,y)\in X\times X:x\subseteq y\}, $$
>
> then $\subseteq_X$ is a reflexive, transitive and antisymmetric [relation](/codex/R) on $X$, which menas $\subseteq_X$ is a [partial order](/codex/PO) on $X$.
>
> Notice that we need to limit the relation onto a set $X$ in order for it to form a set.

> <span class="math-h">PROP-SUB-PIREF. Irreflexivity of $\subset$.</span>
>
> For any $X$,
>
> $$ X\not\subset X. $$

> <span class="math-h">PROP-SUB-PTR. Transitivity of $\subset$.</span>
>
> For any $X$, $Y$ and $Z$,
>
> $$ X\subset Y\land Y\subset Z \enspace\Rightarrow\enspace X\subset Z. $$

> <span class="math-h">PROP-SUB-PASYM. Asymmetry of $\subset$.</span>
>
> For any $X$ and $Y$,
>
> $$ X\subset Y \enspace\Rightarrow\enspace Y\not\subset X. $$

> <span class="math-h">PROP-SUB-SPO.</span>
>
> For any $X$, if we let
>
> $$ {\subset_X} = \{(x,y)\in X\times X:x\subset y\}, $$
>
> then $\subset$ is an irreflexive, transitive and asymmetric [relation](/codex/R) on $X$, which means $\subset$ is a [strict partial order](/codex/PO) on $X$.
>
> Notice that we need to limit the relation onto a set $X$ in order for it to form a set.
