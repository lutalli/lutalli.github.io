> **DEF-IT. Intersection.**{:.codex-h}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - Let $C$ be a [class](/codex/CLS) of sets. The **intersection** of $C$ is
>
>   $$ \bigcap C = \left\{x : (\forall X\in C:x\in X)\right\}. $$
>
>   This is a set: $\bigcap C\subseteq X$ for any $X\in C$.
>
> - We define
>
>   $$ \begin{align*}
        A\cap B &= \bigcap\{A,B\}, \\
        A\cap B\cap C &= (A\cap B)\cap C, \\
        A\cap B\cap C\cap D &= (A\cap B\cap C)\cap D, \\
    \end{align*} $$
>
>   and so on.

---

> **PROP-IT-EMP.**{:.codex-h}
>
> $$ \bigcap\varnothing = \varnothing. $$

> **PROP-IT-EMP2.**{:.codex-h}
>
> For any $X$,
>
> $$ X\cap\varnothing = \varnothing. $$

> **PROP-IT-SLF.**{:.codex-h}
>
> For any $X$,
>
> $$ X\cap X = X. $$

> **PROP-IT-COM. Commutativity.**{:.codex-h}
>
> For any $A$ and $B$,
>
> $$ A\cap B = B\cap A. $$

> **PROP-IT-ASS. Associativity.**{:.codex-h}
>
> For any $A$, $B$ and $C$,
>
> $$ (A\cap B)\cap C = A\cap (B\cap C). $$
