> **DEF-PO. Partial Order.**{:.codex-h}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - A **partial order** is an [antisymmetric](/codex/RP#DEF-RP) [preorder](/codex/PRO), i.e. a [binary relation](/codex/R) that is reflexive, transitive and antisymmetric.
>
> - A **strict partial order** is a binary relation that is irreflexive, transitive and asymmetric.

---

> **PROP-PO-ASY.**{:.codex-h}
>
> Irreflexivity and transitivity imply asymmetry. Thus, a relation is a strict partial order as long as it is irreflexive and transitive.

**Proof.**{:.run-in}Let $\sim$ be an irreflexive and transitive relation on $X$. Let $x$, $y\in X$ such that $x\sim y$. If $y\sim x$, then $x\sim x$ by transitivity, contradicting irreflexivity.<qed />

> **PROP-PO-SPO.**{:.codex-h}
>
> $\preceq$ is a partial order if and only if $\prec$ is a strict partial order.
