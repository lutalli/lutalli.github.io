> **DEF-MAX.**{:.codex-h}
>
> Let $(X,\preceq)$ be a [partially ordered set](/codex/PO).
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - If $X$ has a [greatest element](/codex/GR), then it is unique and is called **the greatest element** / **maximum** of $X$, denoted by $\max X$.
>
> - If $X$ has a least element, then it is unique and is called **the least element** / **minimum** of $X$, denoted by $\min X$.

**Proof (Uniqueness).**{:.run-in}If both $a$ and $a'$ are greatest elements of $X$, $a'\preceq a$ and $a\preceq a'$, so $a=a'$ by antisymmetry. The uniqueness of the least element follows analogously.<qed />
