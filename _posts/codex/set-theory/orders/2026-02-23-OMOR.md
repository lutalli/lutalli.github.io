> **DEF-OMOR. Order Morphisms.**{: .codex-h }
>
> {: .no-indent }
> - Let $(X,\preceq)$ and $(Y,\preceq')$ be two [preordered sets](/codex/PO) and $f:X\to Y$ be a [function](/codex/F). $f$ is an **order homomorphism**, also called **order-preserving**, if for all $a$, $b\in X$,
>
>   $$ a\preceq b \enspace\Rightarrow\enspace f(a)\preceq' f(b). $$
>
>   If $\preceq$ and $\preceq'$ are [total](/codex/TO), $f$ is also called **increasing**.
>
> - An **order isomorphism** is a [bijective](/codex/BIJ) order homomorphism.
>
> - An **order endomorphism** on $X$ is an order homomorphism $f:X\to X$.
>
> - An **order automorphism** is an order endomorphism that is an order isomorphism.
>
> - $(X,\preceq)$ and $(Y,\preceq')$ are **isomorphic**, if an order isomorphism between them exists.
>

> **DEF-OMOR-OR.**{: .codex-h }
>
> Let $(X,\preceq)$ and $(Y,\preceq')$ be two preordered sets and $f:X\to Y$ be a function. $f$ is called **order-reversing** / **antitone**, if for all $a$, $b\in X$,
>
> $$ a\preceq b \enspace\Rightarrow\enspace f(b)\preceq' f(a). $$
>
> If $\preceq$ and $\preceq'$ are total, $f$ is also called **decreasing**.

> **DEF-OMOR-S.**{: .codex-h }
>
> Order morphims are defined analogously for [strict partial orders](/codex/PO). In addition:
>
> If $f:X\to Y$ is a homomorphism between two strictly totally ordered sets $(X,\prec)$ and $(Y,\prec')$, i.e. for all $a$, $b\in X$,
>
> $$ a\prec b \enspace\Rightarrow\enspace f(a)\prec' f(b), $$
>
> then $f$ is also called **strictly increasing**. Similarly, if $f$ is order-reversing, i.e. for all $a$, $b\in X$,
>
> $$ a\prec b \enspace\Rightarrow\enspace f(b)\prec' f(a), $$
>
> then $f$ is also called **strictly decreasing**.
