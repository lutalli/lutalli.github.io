> <span class="math-h">DEF-ODRP. Order-Preserving and Order-Reversing Function.</span>
>
> Let $(X,\leq)$ and $(Y,\leq')$ be two [partially ordered sets](/codex/PO) and $f:X\to Y$ be a [function](/codex/F).
>
> - $f$ is **order-preserving** / **monotone** / **isotone** / an **order homomorphism**, if for all $a$, $b\in X$,
>
>   $$ a\leq b \enspace\Rightarrow\enspace f(a)\leq' f(b). $$
>
>   If $\leq$ and $\leq'$ are additionally [total](/codex/TPO), then $f$ is also called **increasing**.
>
> - $f$ is called **order-reversing** / **antitone**, if for all $a$, $b\in X$,
>
>   $$ a\leq b \enspace\Rightarrow\enspace f(b)\leq' f(a). $$
>
>   If $\leq$ and $\leq'$ are additionally total, then $f$ is also called **decreasing**.
