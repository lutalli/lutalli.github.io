> {{ "DEF-MOR:Morphisms" | codexify }}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - Generally, a **homomorphism** / **morphism** is a structure-preserving [function](/codex/F) between two structured sets.
>
> - A **monomorphism** is an [injective](/codex/INJ) homomorphism.
>
> - An **epimorphism** is a [surjective](/codex/SUR) homomorphism.
>
> - An **isomorphism** is a [bijective](/codex/BIJ) homomorphism.
>
> - An **endomorphism** is a homomorphism from a set and to itself.
>
> - An **automorphism** is a bijective endomorphism.
>
> - The sets of all homomorphisms, monomorphisms, epimorphisms, isomorphisms between $X$ and $Y$ are respectively denoted by
>
>   $$ \operatorname{hom}(X,Y), \quad \operatorname{mon}(X,Y), \quad \operatorname{epi}(X,Y), \quad \operatorname{iso}(X,Y). $$
>
>   The sets of all endomorphisms, automorphisms on $X$ are respectively denoted by
>
>   $$ \operatorname{end}(X), \quad \operatorname{aut}(X). $$
>
> - Two structured sets are **isomorphic**, if an isomorphism between them exists.
