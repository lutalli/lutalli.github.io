> <span class="math-h">DEF-TUP. Tuple ($\mathsf{ZF}$).</span>
>
> - The **ordered pair** / **2-tuple** / **couple** of $a$ and $b$, written as $(a,b)$, is the set
>
>   $$ \{\{a\},\{a,b\}\}. $$
>
> - The **3-tuple** / **triple** of $a$, $b$ and $c$ is
>
>   $$ (a,b,c) = ((a,b),c). $$
>
> - **4-tuples** / **quadruples**, **5-tuples** / **quintuples**, etc. are defined analogously.

> <span class="math-h">REM-TUP.</span>
>
> Notice that the arity is not an intrinsic attribute of a tuple. For example, $(a,b,c)$, the **3-tuple** of $a$, $b$ and $c$, is the **2-tuple** of $(a,b)$ and $c$. Technically, we can not say $(a,b)$ is a 2-tuple, but only a 2-tuple **of $a$ and $b$**.

> <span class="math-h">PROP-TUP.</span>
>
> For any $a_1$, $\cdots$, $a_n$ and $b_1$, $\cdots$, $b_n$ ($n\geq 2$),
>
> $$ (a_1,\cdots,a_n) = (b_1,\cdots,b_n) \enspace\Leftrightarrow\enspace a_1=b_1\land\cdots\land a_n=b_n. $$
