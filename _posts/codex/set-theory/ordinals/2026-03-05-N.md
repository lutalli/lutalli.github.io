> **DEF-N. Natural Numbers / Finite Ordinals.**{:.codex-h}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - We define
>
>   $$ \mathbb{N} = \{n : (\forall X:\text{$X$ is inductive}\rightarrow n\in X)\}. $$
>
>   Or, less formally,
>
>   $$ \mathbb{N} = \bigcap\{X:\text{$X$ is inductive}\}. $$
>
>   By [Axiom of Infinity](/codex/ZF#AX-ZF-INF), there exists an inductive set $X$. By definition, $\mathbb{N}\subseteq X$. Thus, by [Separation Schema](/codex/ZF#AX-ZF-SEP), $\mathbb{N}$ is a set.
>
> - $\omega:=\mathbb{N}$ is an [ordinal](/codex/ORD). When treating $\mathbb{N}$ as a set, the symbol $\mathbb{N}$ is used as usual. When treating it as an ordinal, the symbol $\omega$ is used.
>
> - $n$ is a **natural number** / **finite ordinal** if $n\in\mathbb{N}$.
>
> - We define
>
>   $$ 0=\varnothing, \quad 1=\operatorname{suc}0, \quad 2=\operatorname{suc}1, \quad 3=\operatorname{suc}2, $$
>
>   and so on.
>
> - We define
>
>   $$ \mathbb{N}^+ = \mathbb{N}\setminus\{0\}. $$
