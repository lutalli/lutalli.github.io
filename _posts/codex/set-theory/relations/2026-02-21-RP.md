> **DEF-RP.**{:.codex-h}
>
> Let $\sim$ be a [binary relation](/codex/R) on $X$. We define the following properties of $\sim$:
>
> | Property | Condition |
> | :-: | :-: |
> | Reflexive | $x\sim x$ |
> | Irreflexive / Strict | $x\not\sim x$ |
> | Symmetric | $x\sim y\rightarrow y\sim x$ |
> | Antisymmetric | $(x\sim y\land y\sim x)\rightarrow x=y$ |
> | Asymmetric | $x\sim y\rightarrow y\not\sim x$ |
> | Transitive | $(x\sim y\land y\sim z)\rightarrow x\sim z$ |
> | Connected | $x\neq y\rightarrow(x\sim y\lor y\sim x)$ |
> | Strongly connected | $x\sim y\lor y\sim x$ |
> | Left-unique / One-to-many / Injective | $(x\sim z\land y\sim z)\rightarrow x=y$ |
> | Right-unique / Many-to-one / Functional | $(z\sim x\land z\sim y)\rightarrow x=y$ |
>
> The statements in the *Condition* column are meant to hold either for all $x\in X$; all $x$, $y\in X$; or all $x$, $y$, $z\in X$, accordingly.

> **DEF-RP-T.**{:.codex-h}
>
> Let $\sim$ be a binary relation on $X$ and $Y$.
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - $\sim$ is **left-total** if $\operatorname{dom}{\sim}=X$, i.e.
>
>   $$ \forall x\in X\,\exists y\in Y : x\sim y. $$
>
> - $\sim$ is **right-total** / **onto** / **surjective** if $\operatorname{ran}{\sim}=Y$, i.e.
>
>   $$ \forall y\in Y\,\exists x\in X : x\sim y. $$

> **REM-RP-T.**{:.codex-h}
>
> Left-totality and right-totality are context-based properties, as they depend on the choices of $X$ and $Y$.
