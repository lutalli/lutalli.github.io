> {{ "DEF-EQV:Equivalence Relation" | codexify }}
>
> An **equivalence relation** is a binary relation that is reflexive, symmetric and transitive ([DEF-RP](/codex/RP#DEF-RP)).

> {{ "DEF-EQV-CLS:Equivalence Class" | codexify }}
>
> Let $\sim$ be an equivalence relation on $X$ and let $a\in X$. The **equivalence class** of $a$ with respect to $\sim$ is
>
> $$ [a] = \{x\in X:a\sim x\}. $$

> {{ "DEF-EQV-QUO:Quotient Set" | codexify }}
>
> Let $\sim$ be an equivalence relation on $X$. The **quotient set** of $X$ by $\sim$, or $X$ **modulo** $\sim$, is the set
>
> $$ X/{\sim} = \{[x]:x\in X\}. $$

---

> {{ "PROP-EQV-CLS" | codexify }}
>
> Let $\sim$ be an equivalence relation on $X$ and let $x$, $y\in X$. The following statements are equivalent:
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. $x\sim y$.
> 2. $[x]=[y]$.
> 3. $x\in[y]$.
> 4. $[x]\cap[y]\neq\varnothing$.
