> **DEF-R. Relation.**{:.codex-h}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - A **2-ary** / **binary relation** on $X$ and $Y$ is a subset of the [Cartesian product](/codex/CAR) $X\times Y$. 
>
> - A **3-ary** / **ternary relation** on $X$, $Y$ and $Z$ is a subset of $X\times Y\times Z$.
>
> - **4-ary** / **quaternary relations**, **5-ary** / **quinary relations**, etc. are defined analogously.
>
> - An $n$-ary relation on $X$ ($n\geq 2$) is a subset of $X^n$.
>
> Given an $n$-ary relation $R$ ($n\geq 2$), we can write
>
> $$ R(x_1,\cdots,x_n) \quad\text{for}\quad (x_1,\cdots,x_n)\in R. $$
>
> If $R$ is a binary relation, we can also write
>
> $$ x\,R\,y \quad\text{for}\quad R(x,y). $$

> **REM-R-AR.**{:.codex-h}
>
> Since the arity of a tuple is a context-based property ([REM-TUP-AR](/codex/TUP#REM-TUP-AR)), so is the arity of a relation. For example, a ternary relation on $X$, $Y$ and $Z$ could also be seen as a binary relation on $X\times Y$ and $Z$.

> **DEF-R-DOM. Domain.**{:.codex-h}
>
> The **domain** of a binary relation $R$ is
>
> $$ \operatorname{dom}R = \left\{x:(\exists y:x\,R\,y)\right\}. $$
>
> This is a set:
>
> $$ \operatorname{dom}R \subseteq \bigcup\bigcup R. $$

> **DEF-R-RAN. Range.**{:.codex-h}
>
> The **range** of a binary relation $R$ is
>
> $$ \operatorname{ran}R = \left\{y:(\exists x:x\,R\,y)\right\}. $$
>
> This is a set:
>
> $$ \operatorname{ran}R \subseteq \bigcup\bigcup R. $$

> **DEF-R-FLD. Field.**{:.codex-h}
>
> The **field** of a binary relation $R$ is
>
> $$ \operatorname{field}R = \operatorname{dom}R\cup\operatorname{ran}R. $$

---

> **DEF-R-EMP. Empty Relation.**{:.codex-h}
>
> For any sets $X_1$, $\cdots$, $X_n$ ($n\geq 2$), $\varnothing$ is the **empty relation** on $X_1$, $\cdots$, $X_n$.

> **DEF-R-UNI. Universal Relation.**{:.codex-h}
>
> For any sets $X_1$, $\cdots$, $X_n$ ($n\geq 2$), $X_1\times\cdots\times X_n$ is the **universal relation** on $X_1$, $\cdots$, $X_n$.
