> {{ "DIV#DEF:Divisor" | cod }}
>
> Let $n$, $m\in\N$. $n$ **divides** $m$, written
>
> $$ n\divides m, $$
>
> if there exists $k\in\N$ such that
>
> $$ m = kn. $$

- Instead of "$n$ divides $m$", we also say
    - $n$ is a **divisor / factor** of $m$;
    - $m$ is a **multiple** of $n$;
    - $m$ is **divisible** by $n$.

---

> {{ "DIV#PROP-ZERO" | cod }}
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. For all $n\in\N$,
>
>    $$ 0 \divides n. $$
>
> 2. For all $n\in\N^+$,
>
>    $$ n \ndivides 0. $$
