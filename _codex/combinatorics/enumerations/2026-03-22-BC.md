> {{ "DEF-BC:Binomial Coefficient" | codexify }}
>
> For $n$, $k\in\N$ with $n\geq k$, we define the **binomial coefficient**
>
> $$ \binom{n}{k} = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}. $$
>
> Arranging the values of $\binom{n}{k}$ into a triangular array gives **Pascal's Triangle**:
>
> $$ \begin{gather*}
      1 \\
      1 \quad 1 \\
      1 \quad 2 \quad 1 \\
      1 \quad 3 \quad 3 \quad 1 \\
      1 \quad 4 \quad 6 \quad 4 \quad 1 \\
      1 \quad 5 \quad 10 \quad 10 \quad 5 \quad 1 \\
      \vdots
  \end{gather*} $$

---

> {{ "PROP-BC-REC" | codexify }}
>
> For any $n$, $k\in\N^+$ with $n\geq k$,
>
> $$ \binom{n}{k} = \binom{n-1}{k-1} + \binom{n-1}{k}. $$

**Proof.**{:.run-out}

$$ \begin{align*}
    \binom{n}{k} &= \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!} = \frac{(n-1)!}{k![(n-1)-k]!}\cdot\frac{n}{n-k} \\
    &= \frac{(n-1)!}{k![(n-1)-k]!}\cdot\left[\frac{k}{n-k}+1\right] \\
    &= \frac{(n-1)!}{k![(n-1)-k]!}\cdot\frac{k}{n-k}+\frac{(n-1)!}{k![(n-1)-k]!} \\
    &= \frac{(n-1)!}{(k-1)![(n-1)-(k-1)]!}+\frac{(n-1)!}{k![(n-1)-k]!} \\
    &= \binom{n-1}{k-1} + \binom{n-1}{k}.
\end{align*} $$

> {{ "PROP-BC-SYM" | codexify }}
>
> For any $n$, $k\in\N$ with $n\geq k$,
>
> $$ \binom{n}{k} = \binom{n}{n-k}. $$

**Proof.**{:.run-out}

$$ \binom{n}{k} = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!} = \frac{n!}{[n-(n-k)]!(n-k)!} = \binom{n}{n-k}. $$
