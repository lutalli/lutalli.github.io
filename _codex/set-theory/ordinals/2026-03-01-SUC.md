> {{ "SUC#DEF:Ordinal Successor" | cod }}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - The **successor** of an ordinal $\alpha$ is the ordinal
>
>   $$ \alpha+1 = \alpha\cup\{\alpha\}. $$
>
> - $\alpha$ is a **successor ordinal**, if $\alpha=\beta+1$ for some ordinal $\beta$.

---

> {{ "SUC#PROP-INF" | cod }}
>
> For any ordinal $\alpha$,
>
> $$ \alpha+1 = \inf\{\beta:\beta>\alpha\}. $$

> {{ "SUC#PROP-CARD" | cod }}
>
> For any infinite ordinal $\alpha$,
>
> $$ \lvert\alpha+1\rvert = \lvert\alpha\rvert. $$

**Proof.**{:.run-in}By [EQU#PROP-SUB](/codex/EQU#PROP-SUB) and [Schröder-Bernstein Theorem](/codex/EQU#PROP-SB), it is sufficient to show that $\lvert\alpha+1\rvert\leq\lvert\alpha\rvert$:

$$ f : \alpha+1\to\alpha, \, \xi\mapsto\begin{cases}
  0, & \text{if $\xi=\alpha$}; \\
  \xi+1, & \text{if $\xi<\omega$}; \\
  \xi, & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases} $$

is an injection.<qed />
