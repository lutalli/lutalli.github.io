> {{ "DEF-SEQ-T:Transfinite Sequence" | codexify }}
>
> A **transfinite sequence** is a [function](/codex/F) whose domain is an [ordinal](/codex/ORD).
>
> Let $s:\alpha\to X$ be a function for some ordinal $\alpha$.
>
> - $s$ is called a transfinite sequence **in** $X$.
>
> - $\alpha$ is called the **length** of $s$; $s$ an $\alpha$-sequence.
>
> - We also denote the transfinite sequence by
>
>   $$ \langle s_\xi : \xi<\alpha\rangle \quad\text{or}\quad \langle s_\xi\rangle_{\xi<\alpha}, $$
>
>   where $s_\xi$ stands for $s(\xi)$.

> {{ "DEF-SEQ-I:Infinite Sequence" | codexify }}
>
> An **infinite sequence** is an $\omega$-sequence.
>
> We also denote an infinite sequence by
>
> $$ \langle s_n : n\in\N\rangle \quad\text{or}\quad \langle s_n\rangle_{n\in\N}. $$

> {{ "DEF-SEQ-F:Finite Sequence" | codexify }}
>
> A **finite sequence** is an $n$-sequence for some [natural number](/codex/N) $n$.

> {{ "DEF-SEQ-EXT:Extension" | codexify }}
>
> If $s$ is an $\alpha$-sequence, we write
>
> $$ s^\frown x = s\cup\{(\alpha,x)\}. $$
