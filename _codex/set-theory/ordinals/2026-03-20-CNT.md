> {{ "DEF-CNT:Countable and Uncountable Set" | codexify }}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - $X$ is **countable** if there is an [injection](/codex/INJ) from $X$ to $\N$, i.e.
>
>   $$ \lvert X\rvert \leq \aleph_0. $$
>
> - $X$ is **countably infinite** if there is a [bijection](/codex/BIJ) from $X$ onto $\N$, i.e.
>
>   $$ \lvert X\rvert = \aleph_0. $$
>
> - $X$ is **uncountable** if it is not countable. Is $\mathsf{AC}$ assumed, $X$ is uncountable if and only if
>
>   $$ \lvert X\rvert > \aleph_0. $$
