> {{ "DEF-CA:Cardinal Number" | codexify }}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - An ordinal $\alpha$ is a **cardinal number** / **cardinal** if
>
>   $$ \forall\beta<\alpha:\beta\lnequ\alpha. $$
>
> - The class of all cardinals is denoted by $\Card$.
>
> - Every natural number is a cardinal and is called a **finite cardinal**.
>
> - A cardinal is **infinite** if it is not finite.

---

> {{ "PROP-CA-P" | codexify }}
>
> $\Card$ is a proper class.

---

> {{ "PROP-CA-LO" | codexify }}
>
> Every infinite cardinal is a limit ordinal.

**Proof.**{:.run-in}By [PROP-SUC-CARD](/codex/SUC#PROP-SUC-CARD).<qed />

> {{ "PROP-CA-G" | codexify }}
>
> For every ordinal $\alpha$ there is a cardinal greater than $\alpha$.
