> {{ "CARD#DEF:Cardinality" | cod }} <span class="adjunctive">$\limp\AC$</span>
>
> If we assume $\AC$, then every set $X$ can be well-ordered ([WOT#PROP](/codex/WOT#PROP)). By [OT#PROP](/codex/OT#PROP), there is at least one ordinal that is equinumerous to $X$. We define the **cardinality** of $X$ as the least such ordinal:
>
> $$ \lvert X\rvert = \min\{\alpha\in\Ord:X\equ\alpha\}. $$

---

> {{ "CARD#PROP-EQU" | cod }}
>
> For any $X$ and $Y$:
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. $$ X\equ Y \enspace\lrimp\enspace \lvert X\rvert=\lvert Y\rvert. $$
> 2. $$ X\lequ Y \enspace\lrimp\enspace \lvert X\rvert\leq\lvert Y\rvert. $$
> 3. $$ X\lnequ Y \enspace\lrimp\enspace \lvert X\rvert<\lvert Y\rvert. $$
