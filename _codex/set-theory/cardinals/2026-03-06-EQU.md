> {{ "DEF-EQU:Equinumerosity" | codexify }}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - $X$ and $Y$ are **equinumerous**, written $X\equ Y$, if there exists a [bijection](/codex/BIJ) from $X$ onto $Y$.
>
> - $X\lequ Y$ if there exists an [injection](/codex/INJ) from $X$ to $Y$.
>
> - $X\lnequ Y$ if $X\lequ Y$ and $X\not\equ Y$.

---

> {{ "PROP-EQU-REF:Reflexivity of $\lequ$" | codexify }}
>
> For any $X$,
>
> $$ X\lequ X. $$

> {{ "PROP-EQU-TR:Transitivity of $\lequ$" | codexify }}
>
> For any $X$, $Y$ and $Z$,
>
> $$ X\lequ Y \,\land\, Y\lequ Z \enspace\rimp\enspace X\lequ Z. $$

> {{ "PROP-EQU-ATS:Antisymmetry of $\lequ$ (Schröder-Bernstein Theorem)" | codexify }}
>
> For any $X$ and $Y$,
>
> $$ X\lequ Y \,\land\, Y\lequ X \enspace\rimp\enspace X\equ Y. $$

> {{ "PROP-EQU-CON:Connectivity" | codexify }} \[$\lrimp\AC$\]
>
> For any $X$ and $Y$,
>
> $$ X\lequ Y \enspace\lor\enspace Y\lequ X. $$

As a result:

> {{ "PROP-EQU-PO" | codexify }}
>
> $\lequ$ is a [partial order](/codex/PO). If $\AC$ is assumed, it is further a [total order](/codex/TO).

---

> {{ "PROP-EQU-SUB" | codexify }}
>
> If $X\subseteq Y$, then $X\lequ Y$.

**Proof.**{:.run-in}$\id_X:X\to Y$ is an injection.<qed />
