> {{ "DEF-IT:Intersection" | codexify }}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - Let $C$ be a [class](/codex/CLS) of sets. The **intersection** of $C$ is
>
>   $$ \bigcap C = \left\{x : (\forall X\in C:x\in X)\right\}. $$
>
>   This is a set: $\bigcap C\subseteq X$ for any $X\in C$.
>
> - We write
>
>   $$ \begin{align*}
        A\cap B &= \bigcap\{A,B\}, \\
        A\cap B\cap C &= (A\cap B)\cap C, \\
        A\cap B\cap C\cap D &= (A\cap B\cap C)\cap D \\
    \end{align*} $$
>
>   and so on.

---

> {{ "PROP-IT-EMP" | codexify }}
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. $$ \bigcap\varnothing = \varnothing. $$
>
> 2. For any $X$,
>
>    $$ X\cap\varnothing = \varnothing. $$

> {{ "PROP-IT-IPO:Idempotence" | codexify }}
>
> For any $X$,
>
> $$ X\cap X = X. $$

> {{ "PROP-IT-COM:Commutativity" | codexify }}
>
> For any $A$ and $B$,
>
> $$ A\cap B = B\cap A. $$

> {{ "PROP-IT-ASS:Associativity" | codexify }}
>
> For any $A$, $B$ and $C$,
>
> $$ (A\cap B)\cap C = A\cap (B\cap C). $$

As a result:

> {{ "PROP-IT-MO" | codexify }}
>
> For any set $X$, $(\powerset(X),\cap)$ is an abelian semigroup.
