> {{ "DEF-SUP:Supremum and Infimum" | codexify }}
>
> Let $(X,\preceq)$ be a [partially ordered set](/codex/PO) and $A\subseteq X$.
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - If the [least](/codex/MAX) [upper bound](/codex/BND) of $A$ exists, it is called the **supremum** of $A$ and denoted by $\sup A$.
>
> - If the greatest lower bound of $A$ exists, it is called the **infimum** of $A$ and denoted by $\inf A$.

---

> {{ "PROP-SUP-MAX" | codexify }}
>
> Let $(X,\preceq)$ be a partially ordered set and $A\subseteq X$. If $\sup A$ exists, the following statements are equivalent:
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. $\sup A\in A$.
> 2. $\sup A=\max A$.
> 3. $\max A$ exists.
>
> If $\inf A$ exists, the following statements are equivalent:
>
> {:.par-upper-roman start="4"}
> 1. $\inf A\in A$.
> 2. $\inf A=\min A$.
> 3. $\min A$ exists.
