> {{ "DEF-CP:Composition" | codexify }}
>
> Let $R$ and $S$ be [binary relations](/codex/R). Then
>
> $$ S\circ R = \{(x,z):(\exists y:x\,R\,y\land y\,S\,z)\} $$
>
> is also a binary relation and is called the **composition** of $R$ and $S$.
>
> $S\circ R$ is a set by [Separation Schema](/codex/ZF#AX-ZF-SEP):
>
> $$ S\circ R \subseteq \dom R\times\ran S. $$

---

> {{ "PROP-CP-ID" | codexify }}
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. For any binary relation $R$,
>
>    $$ R\circ\id_{\dom R} = \id_{\ran R}\circ R = R. $$
>
> 2. For any binary relation $R$ on $X$,
>
>    $$ R\circ\id_X = \id_X\circ R = R. $$

> {{ "PROP-CP-ASS:Associativity" | codexify }}
>
> For any binary relations $R$, $S$ and $T$,
>
> $$ (R\circ S)\circ T = R\circ(S\circ T). $$

> {{ "PROP-CP-F" | codexify }}
>
> If $f$ and $g$ are [functions](/codex/F) on $X$, then $f\circ g$ is also a function on $X$.

As results:

> {{ "PROP-CP-MO" | codexify }}
>
> For any set $X$, $(\powerset(X\times X),\circ)$ is a [monoid](/codex/MO) with neutral element $\id_X$.

> {{ "PROP-CP-FMO" | codexify }}
>
> For any set $X$, $X^X$ is a submonoid of $\powerset(X\times X)$ with neutral element $\id_X$.

---

> {{ "PROP-CP-CV" | codexify }}
>
> For any binary relations $R$ and $S$,
>
> $$ (R\circ S)^{-1} = S^{-1}\circ R^{-1}. $$
