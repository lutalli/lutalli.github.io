> {{ "DEF-CV:Converse" | codexify }}
>
> Let $R$ be a [binary relation](/codex/R). The **converse** of $R$ is
>
> $$ R^{-1} = \{(y,x):(x,y)\in R\}. $$
>
> This is a set:
>
> $$ R^{-1} \subseteq \ran R\times\dom R. $$

---

> {{ "PROP-CV-IVO:Involutivity" | codexify }}
>
> For any binary relation $R$,
>
> $$ (R^{-1})^{-1} = R. $$

> {{ "PROP-CV-SYM" | codexify }}
>
> A binary relation $R$ is [symmetric](/codex/RCL) if and only if $R=R^{-1}$.

---

> {{ "PROP-CV-FC" | codexify }}
>
> If $A$ and $B$ are [finite](/codex/FIN) and $f:A\to B$ is a [surjection](/codex/SUR), then
>
> $$ \lvert A\rvert = \sum_{b\in B}\big\lvert f^{-1}\big[\{b\}\big]\big\rvert. $$

**Proof.**{:.run-in}Since $f$ is a [function](/codex/F),

$$ A = \bigsqcup_{b\in B}f^{-1}\big[\{b\}\big]. $$

Apply [PROP-DU-CARD](/codex/DU#PROP-DU-CARD).<qed />

> {{ "PROP-CV-FC-C" | codexify }} \[Corollary to [PROP-CV-FC](/codex/CV#PROP-CV-FC)\]
>
> If $A$ and $B$ are finite and $f:A\to B$ is a surjection such that for every $b\in B$,
>
> $$ \big\lvert f^{-1}[\{b\}]\big\rvert = k, $$
>
> then
>
> $$ \lvert A\rvert = k\lvert B\rvert. $$
