> {{ "DIM#DEF:Dimension" | cod }} <span class="adjunctive">$\limp\AC$</span>
>
> Let $V$ be a vector space. By [BS#PROP-EX](/codex/BS#PROP-EX), $V$ has a basis. By [BS#PROP-L](/codex/BS#PROP-L), all bases of $V$ have the same length. This length is called the **dimension** of $V$, written as $\dim V$.

---

> {{ "DIM#PROP-S" | cod }}
>
> Let $V$ be a vector space and $W\subseteq V$ be a subspace.
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. $$ \dim W \leq \dim V. $$
>
> 2. $$ \dim W = \dim V \enspace\rimp\enspace W = V. $$

> {{ "DIM#PROP-SUM" | cod }}
>
> Let $V$ be a vector space and $X$, $Y\subseteq V$ be finite-dimensional subspaces. Then
>
> $$ \dim(X+Y) = \dim X + \dim Y - \dim(X\cap Y). $$
