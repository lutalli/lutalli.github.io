> {{ "DEF-FH:Field Homomorphism" | codexify }}
>
> A **field homomorphism** is a ring homomorphism between two fields.
>
> Namely, a field homomorphism $f:F\to K$ is both a group homomorphism from $(R,+)$ to $(S,+)$ and a group homomorphism from $(R^\*,\cdot)$ to $(S^\*,\cdot)$.

---

> {{ "PROP-FH-A" | codexify }}
>
> Let $f:F\to K$ be a field homomorphism.
>
> {:.par-upper-roman}
> 1. $$ f(1) = 1. $$
>
> 2. $$ f(0) = 0. $$
>
> 3. $$ f(-a) = -f(a) $$
>
>    for every $a\in F$.
>
> 4. $$ f(a^{-1}) = f(a)^{-1} $$
>
>    for every $a\in F^\*$.
>
> 5. $f$ is a monomorphism.

**Proof.**{:.run-out}

- (I): By [DEF-RH (III)](/codex/RH#DEF-RH).

- (II), (III), (IV): By [PROP-GH-A](/codex/GH#PROP-GH-A).

- (V): By (IV) and [PROP-RING-I](/codex/RING#PROP-RING-I),

  $$ \ker f = \{0\}. $$

  By [PROP-RH-MON](/codex/RH#PROP-RH-MON), $f$ is a monomorphism.

&#8203;<qed />
