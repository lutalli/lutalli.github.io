> {{ "ZZ#DEF:Integers" | cod }}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - We define the following equivalence relation on $\N\times\N$:
>
>   $$ (a,b)\sim(x,y) \enspace\lrimp\enspace a+y=b+x. $$
>
>   The set of **integers** is defined as
>
>   $$ \Z = (\N\times\N)/{\sim}. $$
>
> - Addition and multiplication on $\Z$ are defined by
>
>   $$ \begin{align*}
>       [(a,b)]+[(x,y)] &= [(a+x,b+y)], \\
>       [(a,b)]\cdot[(x,y)] &= [(ax+by,ay+bx)].
>   \end{align*} $$
>
> - $\N$ is embedded into $\Z$:
>
>   $$ 0 := [(0,0)] $$
>
>   and for every $n\in\N^+$,
>
>   $$ n := [(n,0)]; \quad -n := [(0,n)]. $$
>
>   Then we can say
>
>   $$ \N\subseteq\Z. $$
>
> - The following defines a well-order on $\Z$:
>
>   $$ [(a,b)]\leq[(x,y)] \enspace\lrimp\enspace a+y\leq b+x. $$
>
> - The set of **non-zero integers** is
>
>   $$ \Z^* = \Z\setminus\{0\}. $$
>
>   The set of **negative integers** is
>
>   $$ \Z^- = \{z\in\Z : z<0\}. $$

---

> {{ "ZZ#PROP-RING" | cod }}
>
> $(\Z,+,\cdot,0,1,\leq)$ is a well-ordered abelian ring.

---

> {{ "ZZ#DEF-NEAT" | cod }}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - If $a$, $b\in\Z$, we write
>
>   $$ \llbra a,b\rrbra = \{k\in\Z:a\leq k\leq b\}. $$
>
>   - If $a\leq b$,
>
>     $$ \llbra a,b\rrbra = \{a,a+1,\cdots,b\}. $$
>
>   - If $b<a$,
>
>     $$ \llbra a,b\rrbra = \varnothing. $$
>
> - If $n\in\Z$, we write
>
>   $$ \llbra n\rrbra = \llbra 1,n\rrbra. $$
>
>   - If $n\leq 0$,
>
>     $$ \llbra n\rrbra = \varnothing. $$
