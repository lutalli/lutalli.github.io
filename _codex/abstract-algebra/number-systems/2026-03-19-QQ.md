> {{ "DEF-QQ:Rational Numbers" | codexify }}
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - We define the following [equivalence relation](/codex/EQV) on $\Z\times\Z^\*$:
>
>   $$ (a,b)\sim(x,y) \enspace\leftrightarrow\enspace ax=by. $$
>
>   The set of **rational numbers** is defined as
>
>   $$ \Q = (\Z\times\Z^*)/{\sim}, $$
>
>   where we write
>
>   $$ \frac{a}{b} = [(a,b)]. $$
>
> - Addition and multiplication on $\Q$ are defined by
>
>   $$ \frac{a}{b} + \frac{x}{y} = \frac{ay+bx}{by} $$
>
>   and
>
>   $$ \frac{a}{b}\cdot\frac{x}{y} = \frac{ax}{by}. $$
>
> - $\Z$ is embedded into $\Q$: For every $z\in\Z$, we write
>
>   $$ z = \frac{z}{1}. $$
>
>   Then we can say
>
>   $$ \N \subseteq \Z \subseteq \Q. $$
>
> - The following defines a [total order](/codex/TO) on $\Q$:
>
>   $$ \frac{a}{b} \leq \frac{x}{y} $$
>
>   if and only if
>
>   $$ (by>0\,\land\,ay\leq bx) \enspace\lor\enspace (by<0\,\land\,ay\geq bx). $$
>
> - The set of **positive rational numbers** is
>
>   $$ \Q^+ = \{q\in\Q:q>0\}. $$
>
>   The set of **non-negative rational numbers** is
>
>   $$ \Q_{\geq 0} = \{q\in\Q:q\geq 0\}. $$
