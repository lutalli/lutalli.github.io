> {{ "DEF-QG" | codexify }}
>
> Let $G$ be a group and $N$ be a normal subgroup. The binary operation $\*$ on $G/N$ defined by
>
> $$ aN*bN = (ab)N $$
>
> is well-defined and $(G/N,\*)$ is a group, called the **quotient group** of $G$ by $N$.

---

> {{ "PROP-QG-B" | codexify }}
>
> Let $G$ be a group and $N$ be a normal subgroup. Then
>
> $$ G/N = N\backslash G. $$

> {{ "PROP-QG-EQV" | codexify }}
>
> Let $G$ be a group and $H$ be a subgroup. We define two equivalence relations on $G$: For all $a$, $b\in G$,
>
> $$ a\sim_H b \enspace\lrimp\enspace ab^{-1}\in H $$
>
> and
>
> $$ a \prerel{H}{\sim} b \enspace\lrimp\enspace a^{-1}b\in H. $$
>
> {:.no-indent}
> - For every $a\in G$,
>
>   $$ [a]_{\sim_H} = aH $$
>
>   and
>
>   $$ [a]_\prerel{H}{\sim} = Ha. $$
>
> - If $H$ is normal, then
>
>   $$ {\sim_H} = {\prerel{H}{\sim}} $$
>
>   and
>
>   $$ G/H = G/{\sim_H}. $$
