> {{ "OG#DEF:Ordered Group" | cod }}
>
> Let $G$ be a group and $\leq$ be an order. $G$ is **ordered** by $\leq$ if multiplication preserves the order, i.e. for all $a$, $b$, $x\in G$,
>
> $$ a\leq b \enspace\rimp\enspace ax\leq bx \,\land\, xa\leq xb. $$
