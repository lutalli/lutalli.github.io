> {{ "DEF-PAR:Parity of Permutation" | codexify }}
>
> Let $\sigma\in\SS_n$ be a [permutation](/codex/SYG) ($n\in\N^+$). The **parity** of $\sigma$ is
>
> $$ \par\sigma = (-1)^k, $$
>
> where
>
> $$ k = \Big\lvert\big\{(i,j)\in\llbra n\rrbra\times\llbra n\rrbra : i<j\,\land\,\sigma(i)>\sigma(j)\big\}\Big\rvert $$
>
> is the number of *inversions* in $\sigma$. $\sigma$ is called an **even permutation** if $k$ is [even](/codex/EVEN) and an **odd permutation** if $k$ is odd.

---

> {{ "PROP-PAR-PROD" | codexify }}
>
> If $n\geq 2$ and $\sigma\in\SS_n$,
>
> $$ \par\sigma = \prod_{1\leq i<j\leq n}\frac{\sigma(i)-\sigma(j)}{i-j}. $$

> {{ "PROP-PAR-HOM" | codexify }}
>
> For any $n\in\N^+$, $\par:\SS_n\to\\{-1,1\\}$ is a [group homomorphism](/codex/GH). We have
>
> $$ \ker\par=\AA_n, $$
>
> the [alternating group](/codex/ALT) of degree $n$.
